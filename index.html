Hi! I'm Muhammad Rafli Raihan,

Hello! Welcome to my digital corner. I’m a tech enthusiast, freelancer, and musician (well, sort of...) based in Jakarta, Indonesia. My days are packed with a random mix of things I love to do and things I absolutely must do. This ranges from coding and freelancing to repairing dying laptops and smartphones, writing songs, and performing on stage with my band. When I just want to unwind, I’m simply surfing the web like everyone else.

As a programmer, my focus lately has been deeply rooted in the open-source world. I’m a passionate Open Source Enthusiast who believes that the best code is the kind that is shared, studied, and improved collectively by the community. On top of that, my love for the digital realm has led me to dive into life as a Video Game Developer. Fusing music, visuals, and logic into an interactive experience is one of my biggest passion projects right now.

This new rendition of my website actually was born out of a unique challenge from my lecturer. I was challenged to build a website layout using strictly table tags (<table>). Yes, we are talking about the ancient ways of web design a glorious era of the past when the younger demographic could actually still afford to buy a house.

To answer that challenge, I decided to go all-in on a 90s-style static HTML aesthetic. While building this site, I imagined myself living in an era before the internet was plagued by brainrot content and addictive social media algorithms (also known as LARPing). Mind you, if you look closely at the left side of the screen, you will still catch a few 4th wall breaks I intentionally threw in there.

So, just pretend you’re surfing the web with me back in 1998. Picture us staring into a CRT monitor powered by a Windows 95 Pentium II PC, winding down after a grueling session of trying to beat Resident Evil 2 on a brand-new, first-generation PlayStation. Enjoy the digital nostalgia trip!
